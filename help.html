How to use

  • For Twitch, Smashcast, and Mixer: Simply select the platform the streamer is streaming on, type in the streamer's username, and then click Go.
  • For YouTube/YouTube Gaming: Select YouTube, enter the Video ID, and click Go. (Username or Channel ID will not work.)

Finding the YouTube/YouTube Gaming Video ID

Look at the URL in the address bar of your browser.
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQw4w9WgXcQ
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dQw4w9WgXcQ&feature=youtu.be
The part in bold and underlined is the Video ID. It's anything after "v=" but before the ampersand (&) if there is one.

If you do not see "v=" at all in the URL (e.g. the URL has "/live" at the end), then you can do one of two things:
  • Go to the streamer's channel page, then click the stream from there. You should get the URL with the Video ID in it.
  • Open the popout chat (click the three dots at the top right of the chat and click "Popout chat"), and the URL of it should have the Video ID in it.
